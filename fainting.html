Fainting, or syncope, occurs when blood flow to the brain temporarily decreases, causing brief loss of consciousness. It is usually not serious, but can sometimes signal an underlying health issue. Stay calm, ensure the person is safe, and help them lie flat with their legs raised to restore blood flow to the brain.

  • Low blood sugar or missed meals
  • Emotional stress or fear
  • Pain or shock
  • Heart or blood pressure problems

  • Lay the person flat on their back
  • Raise their legs about 12 inches to improve blood flow
  • Loosen tight clothing, especially around the neck
  • Ensure fresh air ‚Äî open windows or use a fan
  • Once conscious, encourage them to sit slowly and rest

  • If they injure themselves during the episode

Prevention focuses on staying hydrated, maintaining good circulation, and avoiding triggers like standing too long or skipping meals. Understanding your body‚Äôs signals can help prevent fainting episodes.
